Replicator Dynamics

Consider a finite or infinite population of individuals whose strategies can be categorized into a finite set \(S={1,...,n}\).
Let \(x=(x_1,...,x_n)\) be a vector containing the fractions of individuals with each strategy in \(S\).
The fraction of individuals \(x_i\) using strategy \(i\) can be modelled using the following differential equation: \[\dot{x}_i = x_i\left(f_i(x) - \bar{f}(x)\right),\] where \(f_i(x)\) is the fitness of individuals using strategy \(i \in S\) which depends on the fractions of individuals with each strategy in \(S\), and \(\bar{f}(x)=\sum_{i\in S} f_i(x)x_i\) is the average fitness of the entire population.
